Cookie Policy

Last updated: 3 December 2025

1. About this Cookie Policy

This Cookie Policy explains how Codor Ltd ("Codor", "we", "us", "our") uses cookies and similar technologies on https://codor.uk.

It should be read alongside our Privacy Policy.

2. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work, or work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to site owners.

We may also use similar technologies such as local storage and pixels where appropriate. In this policy, we refer to all of these as "cookies".

3. Types of cookies we use

3.1 Strictly necessary cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function properly and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually set in response to actions you take, such as:

  • Navigating between pages;
  • Setting your privacy preferences;
  • Submitting forms.

You can set your browser to block these cookies, but parts of the site may then not work correctly.

3.2 Performance and analytics cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors use the site so we can measure performance and improve content. For example, they may help us understand:

  • Which pages are visited;
  • How long visitors spend on each page;
  • Which links are clicked.

Where we use analytics tools that set cookies, we aim to configure them in a privacy-conscious way.

We will only use non-essential analytics cookies if you have given your consent through our cookie banner.

3.3 Functionality cookies

These cookies allow the site to remember choices you make (such as preferences) and provide enhanced features. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we use on our pages.

We only use these where they are genuinely needed for functionality and, where required by law, with your consent.

We do not currently use cookies for behavioural advertising on this site.

4. Cookies in use

Our use of cookies is currently limited and focused on essential operation and basic performance insights. As we evolve the site and add tools (for example, analytics or embedded services), we will update this section to describe them in more detail.

Where we implement third-party analytics, those tools may collect:

  • A pseudonymous identifier;
  • Approximate location (country/region);
  • Device and browser type;
  • Pages visited and time spent.

We do not use this information to identify you directly.

5. Managing cookies

You can manage cookies in several ways:

  • Using our cookie banner or preference tools (where implemented) to accept or reject non-essential cookies.
  • Controlling cookies via your browser settings, including:
    • Blocking all cookies;
    • Deleting existing cookies;
    • Configuring notifications when a cookie is set.

Please note that blocking some types of cookies may impact your experience of the site.

For more information, refer to the help section of your browser.

6. Changes to this Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, law or our use of cookies. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date.
